Domain setup

Your domain is your website address, like yourbusiness.com. It should be short, easy to spell, and close to your business name.

1

Choose your website name

Pick a name that matches your business and is easy for customers to remember.

2

Buy the domain

You can buy domains from providers like Namecheap, GoDaddy, Cloudflare, or Google-related domain partners.

3

Connect it to your website

Your domain gets connected to your hosting provider using DNS records.

Tip: Avoid long domains, hyphens, confusing spelling, and names that are hard to say out loud.

Hosting setup

Hosting is where your website files are stored so people can visit your site online. For MintLaunchKit templates, simple static hosting is enough to start.

1

Pick a hosting platform

For simple websites, Vercel is a good starting point because it connects easily to GitHub.

2

Connect your GitHub repository

Your website files live in GitHub, and your host automatically updates the site when you commit changes.

3

Add your custom domain

Once the site is live, connect your domain so customers can visit your real website address.

Tip: Start simple. You do not need a complicated backend until you add dashboards, logins, payments, or customer accounts.

Business email setup

A business email makes your brand look more professional. Instead of using a personal email, you can use something like contact@yourbusiness.com.

1

Choose your email address

Common choices are info@, contact@, hello@, support@, or your name at your domain.

2

Pick an email provider

Popular options include Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Zoho Mail, or email forwarding.

3

Connect email records

Your domain will need email DNS records so messages can send and receive correctly.

Tip: For a new business, hello@yourdomain.com or contact@yourdomain.com is usually a clean choice.

Payments setup

Payment setup lets your website collect deposits, invoices, booking fees, or full online payments. This can be added after your basic site is live.

1

Decide what you want to collect

Examples include deposits, consultation fees, invoices, subscriptions, or full product payments.

2

Choose a payment tool

Stripe, Square, PayPal, and invoice tools can all work depending on the business.

3

Add payment links or checkout

To start simple, use payment links. Later, you can add full checkout pages or customer dashboards.

Tip: Payment links are the easiest first step before building a full custom checkout system.
